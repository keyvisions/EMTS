\chapter{The Quadrants and Their Implications}
\label{ch:fundamental-forces}

Let the pre-measurement state be $z(t_0)=r e^{i\theta_0}$. A projection onto the real axis yields $m_z=\projReal$. Hypothesis: the interaction channel observed in spacetime is tagged by the pre-collapse quadrant of $z(t_0)$.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Quadrant I (\quadEM, $0<\theta_0<\tfrac{\pi}{2}$): Electromagnetic
  \item Quadrant II (\quadWeak, $\tfrac{\pi}{2}<\theta_0<\pi$): Weak
  \item Quadrant III (\quadStrong, $\pi<\theta_0<\tfrac{3\pi}{2}$): Strong
  \item Quadrant IV (\quadGrav, $\tfrac{3\pi}{2}<\theta_0<2\pi$): Gravitational
\end{itemize}

With phase windows $W_f(\theta)$ and coupling $g(\theta,r)$, a cycle-averaged strength at radius $r$ is
\[
\bar g_f(r)=\frac{\omega(r)}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} W_f(\theta)\,g(\theta,r)\,d\theta.
\]
